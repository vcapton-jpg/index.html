EdRAM vs Peers
Where EdRAM aligns, diverges, or stands alone vs the competitive landscape
INSIGHT 01 · CONSENSUS ALIGNMENT
EdRAM aligns with the bullish equity consensus but with stronger Euro conviction
83% of peers are overweight global equities. EdRAM shares this stance but shows a distinctly stronger tilt toward European equities vs US, positioning it alongside DWS and Pictet — and ahead of Amundi's more cautious stance on Euro assets. This creates a commercial narrative around a region-specific recovery play.
EquitiesEuropeAligned
INSIGHT 02 · KEY DIVERGENCE
EdRAM's EUR/USD call is more bullish on the euro than 70% of peers
EdRAM targets EUR/USD at 1.08 vs the peer median of 1.05–1.06. Only UBS AM and Lombard Odier share this view. The divergence reflects EdRAM's confidence in ECB easing impact and eurozone stabilisation — a contrarian stance that could be a powerful sales angle in FR, DE, and IT if validated in H1.
FXDivergenceEUR/USD
INSIGHT 03 · UNIQUE POSITIONING
Water & biodiversity — EdRAM is an outlier, and that is an opportunity
Only Pictet and Lombard Odier have a meaningful water/natural resources allocation in their 2026 outlooks. EdRAM's dedicated Water fund positions it as a first-mover in a theme that <1 in 5 peers are developing. With EU Nature Restoration Law in force, this is underutilised commercial territory across all five markets.
ThematicDifferentiationWater
INSIGHT 04 · BLIND SPOT
European Defence is a growing consensus theme EdRAM has limited exposure to
61% of peers are actively overweighting European defence and security post-Ukraine and NATO commitment upgrades. DWS, Allianz GI, and AXA IM have dedicated positioning. EdRAM's fund range has limited direct exposure here — a potential product gap in DE and FR where this theme resonates most with institutional clients.
DefenceGapProduct
INSIGHT 05 · RATE VIEW
EdRAM's ECB rate forecast is the most dovish in the peer set
EdRAM forecasts ECB at 1.50% by YE 2026 — matching only Pictet, Lombard Odier, UBS AM, and M&G. This implies 4-5 cuts vs the peer median of 3 cuts. This dovish stance supports EdRAM's overweight on European fixed income and provides a compelling narrative for duration extension strategies in fixed income funds.
ECBDurationFixed Income
INSIGHT 06 · THEME STRENGTH
Healthcare conviction is one of EdRAM's strongest differentiators vs broad peer consensus
EdRAM's healthcare overweight aligns only with Pictet and UBS AM at highest conviction. The majority of peers (56%) have a moderate or neutral healthcare view, making EdRAM's dedicated healthcare fund a genuine differentiator — particularly in CH and IT where private banking clients seek theme-specific strategies.

INSIGHT 07 · CHINA CALL
EdRAM's selective China optimism is a minority contrarian view with potential upside
Only 28% of peers have a constructive China view. EdRAM's positive stance on selective China exposure puts it alongside UBS AM in a minority contrarian camp. If Chinese stimulus delivers, EdRAM's EM funds stand to outperform dramatically — a key narrative to pre-position with clients in ES and IT before consensus shifts.
ChinaContrarianEM
INSIGHT 08 · GOLD
Strong gold consensus — EdRAM's multi-asset funds well positioned to capture this theme
78% of peers are overweight gold as a geopolitical hedge and dollar-weakness play. EdRAM's multi-asset funds with commodity exposure are well aligned. This is a cross-selling opportunity: clients in any of the five markets asking about geopolitical protection can be directed to multi-asset strategies with explicit gold exposure.

INSIGHT 09 · SMALL CAP
EU small & mid cap is underplayed by peers — EdRAM's niche could outperform
Only 44% of peers have a constructive view on EU small & mid cap. With ECB rate cuts expected to disproportionately benefit rate-sensitive smaller companies, EdRAM's dedicated European small cap strategies could significantly outperform. This is particularly relevant in FR and DE where small cap domestic champions are widely held by retail investors.

Geopolitical risk is universally flagged but poorly monetised — EdRAM needs a narrative
72% of peers cite geopolitics as the primary tail risk, yet fewer than 30% have explicit portfolio positioning. This represents a structural gap across the industry. EdRAM's ability to articulate a multi-asset geopolitical hedging framework — combining gold, infrastructure, and selective EM — could differentiate Sales conversations in all five markets.
